About Me

Dr. Jitendra Singh | Academic Researcher | Operational Research | Supply Chain Management

Dr. Jitendra Singh is an academic researcher specializing in Operational Research and Supply Chain Management. He holds a PhD, MPhil, and MSc in Operational Research from the Department of Operational Research, University of Delhi. His doctoral thesis focused on optimizing relief supply chain inventory models. His broader research interests encompass mathematical optimization and public health logistics, aiming to solve complex efficiency problems in humanitarian aid and healthcare supply chains.

Beyond his academic research, Jitendra brings nearly five years of significant, industry-relevant experience. Previously, as a Research Consultant at the Institute of Economic Growth (IEG), he provided technical supply chain expertise to the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW) for the Government of India's Anemia Mukt Bharat (AMB) programme. in this capacity, he conducted a national-level supply chain performance analysis for all states of India. His contributions included developing the AMB Index, national performance scorecards, and the AMB T4 mobile application to streamline beneficiary registration and tracking.

Dr. Singh is UGC NET qualified for Assistant Professor in Management. He is an active contributor to the academic community, serving as a referee for various journals and presenting his work at numerous national and international conferences, where he has received a Best Paper Award. His research has been published in reputed peer-reviewed journals, including PLOS ONE, Global Health: Science and Practice, the International Journal of System Assurance Engineering and Management, and the Yugoslav Journal of Operations Research.
